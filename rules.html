Tic Tac Toe

The classic 3-in-a-row strategy game

🎯 Objective

Be the first player to get three of your marks in a horizontal, vertical, or diagonal row on a 3×3 grid.

📜 Rules

  1. The game is played on a 3×3 grid.
  2. Two players take turns — one is X, the other is O.
  3. Players alternate placing their mark in an empty cell.
  4. The first player to get 3 marks in a row (horizontally, vertically, or diagonally) wins.
  5. If all 9 cells are filled and no one has 3 in a row, the game is a draw.

🤖 Game Modes

👥 Play with Friend

Two players take turns on the same device. Player X goes first.

🤖 Play with Bot

Play against the computer. Choose from three difficulty levels:

  • Easy — Bot plays randomly.
  • Medium — Bot blocks your winning moves.
  • Hard — Bot uses optimal strategy (minimax). Nearly impossible to beat!

You can also choose who goes first — you or the bot.

💡 Tips & Strategy

  • If you go first, take a corner — it gives you the most winning opportunities.
  • If your opponent goes first and takes the center, take a corner.
  • Always check if you can win on your next move before blocking.
  • Watch out for forks — positions where you can win in two ways at once.

Minesweeper

Carefully uncover cells without hitting a mine

🎯 Objective

Reveal all cells on the grid that do not contain mines, without detonating any mine.

📜 Rules

  1. Left-click on a cell to reveal it.
  2. If the cell contains a mine, the game is over — you lose.
  3. If the cell is safe, it shows a number indicating how many of its 8 neighboring cells contain mines.
  4. If a cell has 0 adjacent mines, all neighboring safe cells are automatically revealed.
  5. Right-click on a cell to place a flag 🚩 if you suspect it's a mine.
  6. Right-click a flagged cell again to remove the flag.
  7. Your first click is always safe — mines are placed after your first move.
  8. Win by revealing every non-mine cell on the grid.

📈 Difficulty Levels

LevelGrid SizeMines
🍃 Beginner9 × 910

🌐 Number Guide

1 2 3 4 5 6 7 8

Each number tells you how many of the 8 surrounding cells contain a mine. Use these clues to deduce which cells are safe.

💡 Tips & Strategy

  • Start by clicking near the center — it tends to open up larger areas.
  • If a number equals the count of adjacent hidden cells, all those cells are mines — flag them.
  • If a number's adjacent flags equal the number, all remaining hidden neighbors are safe.
  • Use logical deduction, not guessing. Compare numbers between adjacent cells.
  • The mine counter at the top shows total mines minus placed flags.

2048

Slide and merge tiles to reach the 2048 tile

🎯 Objective

Combine tiles with the same number to create a tile with the value 2048. Keep merging to get the highest score possible!

📜 Rules

  1. The game is played on a 4×4 grid.
  2. Use Arrow Keys (or swipe on mobile) to slide all tiles in one direction.
  3. When two tiles with the same number collide, they merge into one tile with double the value.
  4. After each move, a new tile (2 or 4) appears in a random empty cell.
  5. Merged tiles cannot merge again in the same move.
  6. The game is won when you create a 2048 tile — but you can keep playing!
  7. The game is over when no more moves are possible (no empty cells and no adjacent matching tiles).

🎮 Controls

↑ Slide Up
↓ Slide Down
← Slide Left
→ Slide Right

On mobile devices, swipe in any direction to move tiles.

📊 Scoring

  • Your score increases by the value of each newly merged tile.
  • Merging two 16s gives you a 32 tile and +32 points.
  • Your best score is saved locally so you can try to beat it.

💡 Tips & Strategy

  • Pick a corner strategy — keep your highest tile in one corner.
  • Try to keep tiles ordered from largest to smallest along one edge.
  • Avoid moving in all 4 directions constantly — stick to 2-3 directions when possible.
  • Never move your largest tile out of its corner unless absolutely necessary.
  • Build chains — line up tiles so they merge in sequence (e.g., 4 → 8 → 16 → 32).
  • Keep the board as tidy as possible — scattered small tiles clog the grid.
